About

I am a second-year Ph.D. student at the College of Computing and Data Science, Nanyang Technological University, advised by Prof. Alvin Chan Guo Wei. My research explores new ways to combine large language models with domain foundation models, aiming for integrations that are both more efficient and more effective. The current focus is on scientific domains — small molecules and proteins — where structured FM representations can complement LLM reasoning.

Before NTU, I received my B.Eng. in Computer Science (with a joint degree in Smart Finance & Blockchain Finance) from the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC). I have been fortunate to work with researchers at the Shanghai AI Lab and the NUS HPC-AI Lab on evaluation of generative models and on graph dataset distillation.

Multi-modal LLMs AI for Science Efficient Deep Learning Evaluation of Generative Models Dataset Distillation

I am open to all kinds of novel research directions and always excited by unexplored problems. If you have a collaboration in mind — across disciplines or methodologies — feel free to drop me an email to discuss.
